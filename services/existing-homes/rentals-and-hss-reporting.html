Rentals and HSS Reporting

Rentals and HSS Reporting standards are regulations in New Zealand that aim to improve the quality of rental housing and ensure that rental properties are safe and healthy for tenants to live in.

What are Rentals and HSS Reporting standards?

The Rentals standards cover a range of requirements that landlords must meet, including:

  • Providing a written tenancy agreement to the tenant.
  • Ensuring that the property is clean and tidy at the start of the tenancy.
  • Maintaining the property in a reasonable state of repair.
  • Ensuring that the property meets all relevant building codes and standards.
  • Providing smoke alarms and complying with other fire safety requirements.

The HSS (Healthy Homes Standards) reporting standards are a set of minimum requirements for rental properties to ensure they are warm, dry, and well-ventilated. These standards include:

  • Heating: ensuring that the property has a fixed form of heating in the main living area.
  • Insulation: ensuring that the property has adequate ceiling and underfloor insulation.
  • Ventilation: ensuring that the property has sufficient ventilation to reduce moisture and dampness.
  • Moisture ingress and drainage: ensuring that the property has adequate drainage and is free from moisture ingress.
  • Draught stopping: ensuring that the property is free from draughts that could make it cold and damp.

Landlords and property managers are required to comply with these standards and provide a report to tenants outlining the steps they have taken to meet the standards.
