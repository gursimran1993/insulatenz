EECA

EECA stands for the Energy Efficiency and Conservation Authority, which is a New Zealand government agency responsible for promoting energy efficiency, conservation, and the use of renewable energy sources in homes, businesses, and transport. The EECA works towards reducing the country's greenhouse gas emissions and achieving a more sustainable energy future for New Zealand.

Benefits of EECA:

  • Energy savings: EECA initiatives and programs have helped households, businesses, and public organizations save millions of dollars on energy bills through energy-efficient measures, such as insulation, efficient lighting, and heating upgrades.
  • Environmental benefits: EECA's focus on promoting energy efficiency and the use of renewable energy sources helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions and supports the country's efforts to combat climate change.
  • Economic benefits: Energy efficiency and renewable energy measures supported by EECA have created jobs and generated economic benefits for New Zealand, including increased investment in the renewable energy sector.
  • Health benefits: Improving energy efficiency and reducing energy waste can lead to improved indoor air quality, reducing the risk of respiratory illnesses associated with damp, cold, and poorly ventilated homes.
  • Community benefits: EECA's work helps build more sustainable communities by promoting sustainable energy use, reducing energy poverty, and supporting local initiatives that promote energy efficiency and conservation.

Overall, retrofit insulation installation is a cost-effective and beneficial investment for homeowners, providing long-term energy savings, increased comfort, improved health and well-being, and a more sustainable future.
